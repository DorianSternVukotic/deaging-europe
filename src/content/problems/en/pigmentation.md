summary: Sun damage and post-inflammatory melanin clusters cause uneven tone. Tyrosinase inhibitors and pigment-targeted lasers offer the most reliable fading.
solutions:
  - name: Vitamin C (L-ascorbic acid 10–20 %)
    kind: ingredient
    description: Inhibits tyrosinase and brightens within 8–12 weeks. Layer under SPF in the morning.
    evidence: strong
  - name: Azelaic acid 15–20 %
    kind: ingredient
    description: Targets melanin overproduction without irritating darker skin tones — a safer first-line than hydroquinone.
    evidence: strong
  - name: Niacinamide 5 %
    kind: ingredient
    description: Blocks melanosome transfer to keratinocytes. Stacks well with vitamin C.
    evidence: moderate
  - name: IPL photo-rejuvenation
    kind: device
    description: Pulsed light shatters surface pigment over 3–5 sessions. Not safe for Fitzpatrick V–VI skin.
    evidence: strong
